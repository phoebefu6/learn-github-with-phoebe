# Presenter notes - Session 3 · Issues + Projects

## Preflight (10 min before)
- Open courses/03-issues-and-projects.html, click Expand all, set Projector zoom: on.
- Test the triage sorter widget once: click one item into the wrong category, then the right one, so you know the feedback colors.
- Demo repo ready: your own team-handbook with 2-3 issues already filed, one label created, and a Projects board with a card you can drag live.
- Confirm everyone finished Session 2 (they need their team-handbook repo). Have a 60-second catch-up plan: create repo + README, skip the front door.

## Run of show
- 0-3 · Welcome. Ask the room: "Where do your team's asks live right now?" Collect three answers (email, chat, someone's head). That is the villain of the day.
- 3-8 · Part 1, issues. Walk the anatomy SVG zone by zone: title, labels, context + done-looks-like, task-list progress bar, one owner. Say the gloomy-name disclaimer early: issues are tasks, ideas, questions, decisions - not just bugs.
- 8-12 · Part 2, labels/assignees/milestones. Keep it brisk: labels = what kind, assignee = who (exactly one), milestone = by when. Tell the good-first-issue story - a sticker that became a global onboarding system.
- 12-18 · Part 3, Projects. Walk the board SVG left to right, then trace the green tether from the #12 card down to the repo box. Say the sentence twice: "The card IS the issue. The board is just a view." It is the one idea people get wrong forever if missed.
- 18-26 · Apply-along 1. Four issues, three labels, self-assigned, two in the Launch week milestone. Circulate for vague titles; make people say the verb out loud if stuck.
- 26-34 · Apply-along 2. The triage sorter. Let them argue - the URGENT!!! item always splits the room between retitle and close. Debrief with the three triage questions in order: clear ask? owned + labeled? should it even be open?
- 34-40 · Apply-along 3. Build the board, add the 4 issues, drag one to In progress LIVE on the projector before they do - the drag gets a reaction every time. Then the Priority field.
- 40-45 · Quiz (90 seconds) then Q&A. Assign the homework with a straight face: three real task-emails become three issues this week.

## Never cut
- The issue-instead-of-email reframe (Part 1 tip + homework). It is the behavior change the whole session exists for.
- Dragging a card live on the projector. Thirty seconds of theater that makes Projects feel real, not theoretical.

## Cut if long
- The table-view / standup-slice material (it is a self-study card; just point at it).
- The good-first-issue story - compress to one sentence: "one label created a global onboarding convention."
- The Priority field in Apply-along 3; three columns and a drag are the core.

## Expected questions
- "Can we keep using Asana / Jira / Trello?" Yes - nobody migrates mid-quarter. Projects wins when the work lives next to the docs: one address, no sync, no copy-paste. Try one project both ways and compare.
- "Are our issues public?" They follow repo visibility. Public repo = public issues; private repo = invited people only. Same rule as Session 2, no separate switch.
- "Can someone outside the team file an issue?" On a public repo, any GitHub user can open one - that is how open source gets feedback. You can restrict or moderate; private repos take issues from members only.
- "What happens when an issue is done?" Close it. It is never deleted - the number, the discussion and the decision stay searchable forever, and the board card slides to Done on its own.
- "Can one issue live on two boards?" Yes - boards are views, so the same issue can appear on a team board and a launch board. There is still only one issue underneath.
