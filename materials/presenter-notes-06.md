# Presenter notes - Session 6 · Speak fluent GitHub

## Preflight (5 min before)
- Open courses/06-speak-fluent-github.html, Projector zoom 125%.
- Pick the demo repo for the health read: github.com/github/docs open in a tab, plus one deliberately stale repo (find a 3-years-quiet one via trending archives) for contrast.
- Test BOTH widgets once - checklist (tick a few, watch the verdict change) and sorter (one right, one wrong click).
- Have your own handbook repo's Actions tab open, and the Actions Marketplace / New workflow page in another tab.
- Know which robot you will install live (the greetings workflow is the safest crowd-pleaser).

## Run of show
- 0-3 · Welcome. Graduation framing: "Session 1 you panicked at a GitHub link; today you will grade one." Recap the artifact: handbook live since Session 5.
- 3-8 · Part 1 green checks. Point at their own repos: everyone already employs the Pages deploy robot. Verdict reading is the skill: green = safe, red = ask, yellow = wait.
- 8-13 · Part 2 the health read. Do the 90-second read LIVE on github/docs, narrating each stop with a visible timer. Then flash the stale repo: 20k stars, dead 3 years - let the room feel the museum-piece verdict.
- 13-17 · Part 3 phrasebook. Read three sentences from the table aloud, dev-voice; the room translates before you reveal. Keep safety basics as a pointer to the self-study card.
- 17-25 · Apply-along 1 scorecard. github/docs together first, then solo on a repo from their world. Collect verdicts out loud - the disagreements teach.
- 25-32 · Apply-along 2 install a robot. Actions tab → New workflow → greetings → Commit. Then a test PR and WAIT for the robot's comment - do not talk over the yellow dot; the moment it flips is the payoff.
- 32-38 · Apply-along 3 phrasebook sorter. Solo. The clone-vs-fork pair splits the room - let it.
- 38-45 · Q&A + quiz, show of hands. Close with the graduation lap homework and the tech-track teaser.

## Never cut
- The 90-second repo read done live, with a timer - it is the session's core takeaway and the course's data-literacy signature.
- The robot commenting on the test PR - the only live proof that Actions are real, not slideware.

## Cut if long
- Part 3 safety basics - already a self-study card, point at it.
- Apply-along 1 step 3 (compare verdicts) - compress to two volunteers.
- The stale-repo contrast demo - the quiz question covers it.

## Expected questions
- "When is the tech track?" - learn-github-for-builders-with-phoebe is coming; it covers git CLI, branching, conflicts and Actions authoring. Nothing in this track expires when it arrives.
- "Can robots do X?" - Probably. Open the Marketplace and search X together for 30 seconds; browsing it live answers the question better than any list.
- "Do I have to fix a red X?" - No. Your job is to not merge past it and to ask the person who owns that check. Reading the signal IS the fluency.
- "Are the robots free?" - The ones in this session, yes. Public repos get generous free minutes; workplaces manage quotas centrally, so not your problem.
- "What if I accidentally committed a password?" - Treat it as leaked: change the password first, then tell whoever owns the repo. Deleting the file does not unpublish history.
